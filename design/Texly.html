Score-Based Diffusion Priors for
Ill-Posed Inverse Imaging Problems
A. Mercer    L. Nakamura    R. Osei
Institute for Computational Imaging · June 8, 2026
Abstract
We study score-based generative models as learned priors for ill-posed inverse problems. By coupling the reverse diffusion process with a measurement-consistency projection at every denoising step, we obtain reconstructions that are simultaneously data-faithful and perceptually sharp. Across deblurring, inpainting, and compressed-sensing MRI, the proposed sampler improves LPIPS by 18% over unconditional baselines without task-specific retraining.
1  Introduction
Reconstructing a signal x from noisy measurements y = 𝒜x + η is fundamentally underdetermined: many candidate signals explain the same observation. Classical regularizers encode hand-crafted assumptions, whereas a learned prior captures the statistics of natural data directly.
We model the posterior score as the sum of a likelihood and a prior term,
∇x log p(x | y) = ∇x log p(y | x) + ∇x log p(x),(1)
where the intractable prior term is approximated by a time-conditioned score network sθ(x, t) ≈ ∇x log pt(x). The full sampler interleaves an unconditional reverse step with the measurement gradient; see the architecture in the figure below.

2  Method
Let {ti}i=0N be a decreasing noise schedule. Starting from xN ∼ 𝒩(0, σmax2𝙸), each iteration applies the learned score followed by a gradient step on the data term, with step size ζi annealed alongside the noise level.
xi−1 = xi + σi2 sθ(xi, ti) − ζi 𝒜⊤(𝒜xi − y).(2)
We find that decoupling the two step sizes is essential for stability at high measurement noise, mirroring observations in predictor–corrector samplers.
